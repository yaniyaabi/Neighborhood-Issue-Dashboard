Community District Features

Community District 108, also known as the Upper East Side, is home to approximately 220,000 residents, making it one of the most densely populated areas in Manhattan according to the 2020 Census. Foreign-born residents make up 23.6% of the population, while 75.3% identify as white. The district's median household income is $143,940. The district has a mostly middle to high-income population, with many residents working in fields like finance, healthcare, and education. The area is generally safe, with relatively low crime rates and good public services. There are several major hospitals nearby, and the streets are clean and well-maintained. It’s a popular place for families and older adults because of the quiet atmosphere and strong sense of community.

The Upper East Side has easy access to public transit, including the 4, 5, 6, and Q subway lines, as well as many local buses. This makes it simple to get around the city. The neighborhood is close to Central Park and has other outdoor spaces like the East River walkway. There are also many schools, shops, restaurants, and cultural spots like museums along Fifth Avenue. Overall, it’s a comfortable and convenient place to live, with a mix of city life and quieter, more relaxed areas.
