Community District Features

Community District 111, also known as East Harlem, is a historic and vibrant neighborhood in northern Manhattan. According to the 2020 Census, it is home to approximately 117,000 residents, including a diverse mix of working individuals, families, and long-time community members. Foreign-born residents make up 23.5% of the population. The racial and ethnic composition includes 13.7% white, 31.6% Black, and 44.9% Hispanic residents. The district’s median household income is $37,503. While the median household income is lower than in other parts of Manhattan, the community is known for its resilience, strong local networks, and active neighborhood organizations. Public safety has seen improvements in recent years, and community efforts continue to focus on health, housing, and youth programs. The neighborhood includes several medical centers and public facilities that serve a wide range of needs.

East Harlem is served by the 4, 5, and 6 subway lines, multiple bus routes, and the Metro-North station at 125th Street, making it well connected to the rest of the city and beyond. The neighborhood has access to parks and outdoor areas, including parts of Central Park and the Harlem River Greenway. There are numerous public schools, community centers, and small businesses throughout the district. With its mix of historic landmarks, local art, and ongoing development, East Harlem offers a dynamic and evolving environment with a strong sense of place and community life.
